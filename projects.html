ROBOTICS & SOFTWARE

Github: loganw023/Sticks-Up

Hack Club's Outpost (48-hr Hackathon) & Open Sauce

Stick's Up — A 2D Robot Fencing Game

Robotics, Mechanical, Electrical, ML & Software Engineering

Project summary: I was the main CADder and worked significantly on the electricals, especially when repairs were required. Given the task to create an interactable project in 48 hours, Logan Wheatcroft, Mateo Johnson, and I built a 2-dimensional robot fencing game. The hardware was finished in 48 hours, though ML deployment followed afterwards.

Technologies used: 3D printing, Onshape, C++, 9G servo motor, RAMPS 1.4 shield, Arduino MEGA, MG996R Servo, NEMA 17 Stepper, HTD pulley/belt, slide potentiometer, 9V batteries, USB PB Negotiation Board.

Process and challenges: The game needed intuitive controls and simplicity for physics-based training. We opted for a simulation-trained physics model instead of computer vision, enabling math-based hit detection without a massive image database. Position was controlled via a sliding potentiometer alongside three action buttons.

Results and impact: Showcased live at OpenSauce where attendees and Hack Club members played against the trained model and in 2-player modes.

AEROSPACE & AUTONOMY

Github: noahwalshsd/ARC-2025-2026

Aerospace Robotics SRHS Development Team

Quadcopter Pickup & Autonomous Delivery Drone

Robotics, Mechanical, Aerospace, Software, Controls & Electrical

Project summary: To compete in STEM-ED's Aerospace Robotics Competition (ARC), I founded SRHS's second development team. We engineered a quadcopter drone specialized for rapid payload pickup, precision delivery, and autonomous route navigation.

Technologies used: Onshape, C++, VNC, Raspberry Pi OS, Holybro S500 V2 Kit, GPS, Raspberry Pi 5 (8GB) with Camera Module for color recognition, KDE Motors/ESCs, Ardupilot Mission Planner, Pixhawk 6C.

Process and challenges: Operating on a budget ~55% lower than competitor teams required maximizing component error tolerances and lightweight 3D-printed mounts. Despite registration price hikes and hardware failures during testing, we optimized PID loops and vision tracking.

Results and impact: Placed 1st Place at San Diego Regionals (highest overall points across regionals) and 3rd Place at Nationals, alongside winning the Sportsmanship Award.
